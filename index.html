  • Clauses and Phrases

    • Objective: In this lesson, you will learn the difference between clauses and phrases as well as how to identify an independent/ dependent clause. This is a short video that explains the two parts of speech along with examples.
      Clauses: Collection of words that has a subject that is actively doing a verb Independent clause: Simple sentence that can stand on its own. It is a complete thought.
      Ex: 1. Robert handed me his resume.
      Dependent clause: An incomplete though that cannot stand on its own, requires an independent clause to become a complete sentence. Ex: 1. Before Robert handed me his resume
      Phrase: Collection of words that may have nouns or verbs, but it does not have a subject doing a verb. Ex: 1. Everything that glitters isn’t gold.
